Day 1

Section 01 · Increase psychological safety

Prep focus: keep the ramp gentle, concrete, and safe.

Create enough emotional safety for open participation and more honest signal-sharing.

Intro / Context 8 min

Invitation: Let's ground ourselves in why this conversation matters before we step into the team experience more directly.

Facilitator cue: Name the intention warmly and avoid implying anyone must disclose more than they want to.

Transition

Move from shared framing into rapid individual reflection.

1-2-4-All 12 min

Invitation: Think about one moment when collaboration felt easy and energizing. What made that possible?

Flow

Solo reflection -> Pairs -> Foursomes -> Whole-group share -> Harvest

Facilitator cue: harvest only the strongest conditions so the pair work starts from patterns, not from scratch.

Transition

Carry the strongest examples into paired story-sharing.

Appreciative Interviews 20 min

Invitation: Interview your partner about a time when communication in the team felt candid, respectful, and useful. What conditions made that possible?

Review note: shorten this invitation by one clause before final export.

Day 1

Section 02 · Surface communication patterns

Prep focus: keep critique externalized, then convert it into explicit requests.

Make invisible habits visible, then turn requests across functions into explicit commitments and boundaries.

TRIZ 25 min

Invitation: If we wanted to make collaboration and communication fail completely, what would we do?

Flow

Frame the paradox -> list destructive patterns -> notice current reality -> decide what to stop

Facilitator cue: keep it playful enough to create distance from blame, then slow down when the group notices what already happens today.

Transition

Shift from exposing damaging patterns into naming what different functions now need from one another.

What I Need From You 60 min

Core move: clusters state concrete requests to one another, capture them without discussion, then answer only with yes, no, I will try, or whatever.

Facilitator cue: the value comes from clarity and response discipline, not from negotiating every request live.

1.
Frame the challenge

Prompt: explain the challenge, the response options, and the no-discussion rule.

Cue: keep the rules crisp before anyone starts formulating requests.

2.
Cluster needs by role or function

Prompt: reduce your needs to the two requests that matter most.

Cue: push for concrete asks, not complaints or speeches.

3.
State requests around the circle

Prompt: one by one, speak your requests while everyone else only captures them.

Cue: stop responses immediately if people start answering too early.

4.
Prepare responses

Prompt: decide whether each response is yes, no, I will try, or whatever.

Cue: allow short huddles, but keep the response language disciplined.

5.
Respond with discipline

Prompt: repeat the request and answer only with the agreed response language.

Cue: do not let the room slip into explanation or negotiation yet.

6.
Debrief and convert

Prompt: what became clearer, and what should now turn into agreements or next moves?

Cue: use the debrief to convert clarity into concrete follow-through.
